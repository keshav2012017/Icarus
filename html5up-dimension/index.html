WHO AM I

I’m Keshav, a 16-year-old high school student and an independent researcher passionate about aerospace innovation, biomechanics, and human flight. Since childhood, I’ve been fascinated by how birds achieve effortless flight and have dedicated myself to developing Icarus, a project that explores the possibility of a wearable wing system powered by advanced actuation, aerodynamics, and energy recovery. With no formal lab, just a passion for research and engineering, I am working on concepts, simulations, and prototypes to bring this vision to life. Icarus Research is not just a project—it's a step toward redefining what's possible for human flight. I’m always looking to learn, collaborate, and innovate, so if you share the same passion or want to support the project, feel free to reach out!

VISION

Icarus envisions a future where human flight is no longer confined to aircraft but becomes an extension of the human body itself. By combining biomimicry, cutting-edge materials, and advanced actuation systems, Icarus aims to develop a lightweight, efficient, and natural flight system. Designed with adaptability in mind, the system will feature morphing wings that dynamically adjust to flight conditions,feathers that adjust themselves for optimized aerodynamics, and built-in redundancy for safety and reliability. If successful, Icarus could redefine personal aviation, unlocking new possibilities in exploration, mobility, and what it truly means to fly.

DEVLOPMENT

Icarus is currently in the advanced stages of conceptual design for the Mark 1 prototype. While the engineering challenges are immense, every aspect is being carefully refined to ensure a balance between efficiency, strength, and adaptability. The development process focuses on integrating advanced actuation mechanisms, high-performance lightweight materials, and biomimetic wing structures to achieve controlled and sustained human flight. Though complex, every step brings us closer to making this vision a reality—pushing the boundaries of what’s possible in personal aviation.
